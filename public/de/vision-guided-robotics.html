    Hand calipers and sampling miss drift, deform soft parts, and leave no audit trail. GYUTRON gauges every part inline with telecentric optics and laser 3D profiling — height, gap, flatness, and form measured to sub-micron repeatability, traceable to gauge blocks, logged for SPC.

    Telecentric Dimensional GaugingEdge, diameter, gap, and concentricity to ±0.8 µm 2σ. Distortion-free telecentric optics; no contact, no part deformation.
    Laser 3D ProfilingSingle-line and snapshot profilers reconstruct height, step, flatness, and warpage. Z-repeatability to 0.5 µm at full scan speed.
    Surface Form & HeightCoplanarity, ball/lead height, weld bead, glue-line volume. Dense point clouds resolve features 2D imaging cannot reach.
    Traceable & SPC-ReadyGauge-block-referenced calibration with R&R support. Every result time-stamped to the part ID and streamed to SPC.

Architecture

Capture -> Locate -> Plan -> Guide

A deterministic pipeline runs every cycle on a GYUTRON edge controller: image the bin, find each part, plan a reachable grasp, hand coordinates to the robot — closed-loop, under 0.4 s.

1. Capture

Structured-light 3D head and area-scan camera image the full bin. HDR fusion and noise filtering build a dense, gap-free point cloud of stacked parts.

2. Locate

CAD/template matching and 3D segmentation isolate each instance and compute its 6-DoF pose. Confidence scoring rejects partial or occluded candidates.

3. Plan

Grasp ranking selects a reachable, collision-free pick. Bin walls, neighbor parts, gripper geometry, and robot reach are checked before any motion.

4. Guide

Approach, pick, retract, and place coordinates stream to the robot in its base frame. Failed picks trigger automatic re-imaging and the next-best grasp.

Deployment

Trained on your part, not your fixture

Drop in the CAD model, calibrate camera-to-robot, and run. No mechanical re-tooling when parts or robots change.

Run & monitor

Edge controller drives the cycle and logs every pick pose, cycle time, and result for traceability and continuous tuning.

Case Study

Random bin picking of shiny machined parts for CNC loading

Challenge
A precision machining shop fed CNC lathes by hand from bins of chrome-plated steel shafts dumped in random orientations. The reflective, partially overlapping parts defeated an earlier 2D-only system, and hard tooling could not handle 40+ part variants with frequent changeovers. Manual loading capped uptime and exposed operators to sharp, oily stock.
Approach
A GYUTRON cell paired a blue-light structured-light 3D head with an area-scan camera and a robot-mounted edge controller. HDR multi-exposure capture recovered clean point clouds off the chrome surfaces; CAD matching computed 6-DoF poses, and grasp planning checked bin walls, neighbor parts, and gripper clearance before each pick. Coordinates streamed to the existing robot in its base frame, with automatic re-imaging on any missed grasp.
Result
Sustained > 99.5% first-attempt pick success across the bin, including the last layer, at a 0.38 s locate-and-plan cycle and < 0.1 mm pose accuracy. Changeover to a new part variant dropped from hours of re-fixturing to a CAD import. The cell ran lights-out across two shifts, freeing operators from manual loading and lifting CNC utilization.
