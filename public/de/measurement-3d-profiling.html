    Identification & TraceabilityFrom mark to traceable record

    Low-contrast DPM, damaged labels, and high-speed lines push hand scanners and fixed readers past their limits — and a single no-read breaks the genealogy chain. GYUTRON reads 1D/2D/DPM codes, OCR text, and RFID tags, then links each ID into an end-to-end track-and-trace record from raw material to shipped unit.

    1D/2D/DPM code readingData Matrix, QR, PDF417, and all common 1D codes. Dot-peen and laser DPM on metal, PCB, and curved parts decoded under glare and tilt.
    OCR / OCV text readingReads printed and engraved lot, date, and serial strings. Verifies characters against expected format; rejects malformed or missing marks.
    RFID + barcode captureUHF/HF RFID handhelds and rugged scanners for cartons, totes, and pallets. Bulk-read tagged loads where line-of-sight to a label fails.
    End-to-end genealogyEach read links part, process, operator, and parent assembly. Full forward/backward trace; scoped recall by lot, shift, or component batch.

How it works

Sense → Profile → Gauge → Record

A measurement cell built from GYUTRON optics, lasers, and edge compute — calibrated to traceable standards and wired into your quality system end to end.

1. Sense & Trigger

Encoder or sensor triggers capture as the part enters frame. Telecentric optics and white-light or laser illumination freeze the feature without motion blur.

2. Profile & Reconstruct

Laser profiler sweeps the surface; edge controller fuses scans into a calibrated 3D point cloud and aligns it to the part datum.

3. Gauge & Decide

Dimensions extracted against the CAD-derived tolerance set. Pass / fail / rework decided in under 60 ms and signaled to the line PLC.

4. Record & Trace

Each measurement, overlay image, and verdict is logged to the part ID and pushed to SPC / MES for trend control and audit recall.

Case Study

Sealing-face flatness gauged inline on a machined casting line

Challenge
A powertrain machining line released castings on a 1-in-30 sample using hand depth gauges. Sealing-face flatness drift between samples reached the customer as leak failures, and contact gauging marked soft aluminum faces.
Approach
A GYUTRON laser profiler scanned each sealing face inline, reconstructing a 3D surface and computing flatness against the datum plane in under 50 ms. Telecentric gauges added bore diameter and bolt-hole spacing in the same cell. All results were referenced to certified gauge blocks and streamed to SPC keyed to the casting serial.
Result
100% of faces gauged with 0.6 µm flatness repeatability. Flatness drift now triggers a tool-wear alarm before parts go out of tolerance, leak escapes dropped to near zero, and every casting carries a recallable measurement record.
